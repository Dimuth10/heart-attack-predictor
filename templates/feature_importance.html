How Feature Importance Works

The Random Forest model consists of 100 decision trees, each trained on a random subset of the data. Feature importance is measured using Gini impurity — how much each feature reduces uncertainty across all trees.

A higher importance score means the model relies more heavily on that feature when deciding whether a patient is at high or low risk of a heart attack. All scores sum to 1.0 (100%).
