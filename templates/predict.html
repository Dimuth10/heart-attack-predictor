You can measure your heart rate at home using just your fingers and a clock.

1
Place your index and middle fingers on the inside of your wrist just below the thumb, or on the side of your neck beside the windpipe. Never use your thumb.
2
Press gently until you feel a pulse beat. Look at a clock or timer.
3
Count the beats for 30 seconds then multiply by 2 to get your beats per minute (bpm).
4
For maximum heart rate, measure after climbing stairs or a short brisk walk. Your resting rate is measured after sitting quietly for 5 minutes.
5
Normal resting heart rate is 60–100 bpm. Maximum heart rate during activity is approximately 220 minus your age.

If you have a smartphone, many free apps can measure heart rate using your camera flash.
